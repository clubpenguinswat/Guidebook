Armies Explained: Tournaments

ARMY LEAGUE APPLICATION

The information presented in this article is published in respect to the Club Penguin Armies (CPA) league. This means that all information in this article has been formulated keeping their system and rules in mind. Data might not be accurately applicable in other army leagues.

Tournaments can be seen as competition between armies, exclusive of war or battles. They are regular, and help boost engagement within the army community.

WHY DO ARMIES NEED TOURNAMENTS?

Tournaments are competitive and cause interaction among armies. There are tournaments of different styles to bring armies to life and introduce more sportive moves among armies. They may also become a cause of war later on.

WHO ORGANIZES TOURNAMENTS?

Tournaments are organized by an army league. In SWAT's situation, we generally accept tournaments from the popular Club Penguin Armies army league. However, we also participate in tournaments organized by other leagues, including the Club Penguin Army League (CPAL).

How They Work

A tournament is announced by the organizing army league, supposedly days prior. CPA announces the tournament roughly half a month before it begins. However, some popular tournaments take place periodically, allowing anyone to predict what and when the next tournament is taking place. CPA invites a set of armies that are registered with their league to participate in the tournament, and army leaders contact the league administrators confirming their participation in the tourney. After the registration deadline is reached, the army league will announce the list of participating armies, the dates, the seedings if it is a bracket-style tournament, and some important rules. About a week may should still remain till the first battle.

The style of tournaments vary and there are multiple types of tournaments. There are one-battle tournaments, or meetups, such as the New Year Bonanza or the Beach Brawl tournaments. There may also be more popular tournaments, including the Legends Cup and the Christmas Chaos tournaments. These are bracket-style tournaments, which means that they follow a bracket system.

LEGENDS CUP
The Legends Cup tournament is the grandest tournament of the year, and it takes place annually in the summer. It typically occurs in the month of July, which is a month with a lot of activity. This is a bracket-style tournament where the last army standing in the Grand Finals is crowned.

CHRISTMAS CHAOS
The Christmas Chaos tournament is one of the biggest winter tournaments of the year, and it takes place annually in the month of December. It is a period of holidays, including the prominent Christmas festival. This tournament is similar to the Legends Cup, because this a bracket-style tournament where the last army standing in the Grand Finals is crowned.

BEACH BRAWL
The Beach Brawl tournament is a one-day small-medium army community tournament, where all small-medium armies fight each other.
